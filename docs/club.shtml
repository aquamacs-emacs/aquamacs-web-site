Why we need crowd-sourcing

Aquamacs is free software, but producing it costs a lot of time and also some real cash. Over the past 10 years, a single developer has produced and combined the majority of the code that makes Aquamacs unique compared to a standard Emacs implementation. A number of other volunteers helped by means of bug reporting and making helpful suggestions, through documentation and support, and through contributing special packages.

Your donation is an unrestricted gift - we do not promise anything in return. However, we are grateful for your support.

Supporters may, usually shortly before the next major release, receive a (single) thank-you e-mail from the maintainer, including a special update about progress with the project.
